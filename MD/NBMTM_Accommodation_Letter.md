## Patient Information and Diagnosis

Since January 20, 2026, I have been the primary treating physician for
**\[Mother's Full Name\]**, age 69. On that date, the patient suffered a
severe hip fracture requiring immediate surgical intervention and
extensive post-operative care.

The patient currently remains bed-bound and requires **24-hour
assistance for all activities of daily living**.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## Nature of Functional Limitation for the Candidate

Dr. Pete Ngoc Pham has been designated as the **Primary Clinical Support
and Caregiver** for the patient.

Due to the high-acuity nature of the patient's recovery and the absence
of alternative skilled support during the critical six-week period
following the injury (January 20 to late February), Dr. Pham was
physically and cognitively restricted from performing the administrative
tasks required for the standard BCMTM registration window.

His role as primary caregiver, while maintaining full-time clinical
duties at a regional medical center, created a documented scheduling
conflict that obstructed his ability to meet the registration deadline.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## Recommended Accommodation

In my clinical opinion, Dr. Pham is now in a position where the
patient's condition has stabilized enough to allow him to return to
professional certification activities.

I recommend a **30-day extension of the Winter 2026 Test Window** as a
reasonable accommodation. This modification is necessary to allow him
the appropriate timeframe to:

-   Re-engage with clinical materials\
-   Sit for the examination\
-   Fulfill caregiving obligations without compromise

I am available to provide further clarification or review of the
patient's medical records if required by the NBMTM Compliance Office.
